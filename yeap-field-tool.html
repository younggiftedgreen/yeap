Why this matters

This isn't just about saving money. Across the United States, households in Black, Latinx, Indigenous, and low-income communities pay a much larger share of their income on energy than other Americans — for the same kind of home.

3x higher energy burden Low-income households of color often spend three times more of their income on energy bills than the median U.S. household. (American Council for an Energy-Efficient Economy)
60% of U.S. housing built before 1980 Older homes pre-date modern energy codes. They leak more, cost more to heat and cool, and disproportionately serve renters and lower-income owners.
Billions in unclaimed assistance LIHEAP, state weatherization programs, and utility rebates routinely go unused because residents and small businesses don't know about them.

YEAP assessors document what's wrong AND connect residents and small businesses to the resources that can fix it: federal Weatherization Assistance, LIHEAP, ENERGY STAR rebates, and local utility programs.

👨‍🏫 For Educators & Program Staff

YEAP is YGG's flagship youth workforce development program. This field tool is what cohort assessors use during real-time residential and commercial walk-throughs in communities across the country.

DOE alignment

The tool implements the U.S. Department of Energy's Do-It-Yourself Home Energy Assessment framework (energy.gov/energysaver/do-it-yourself-home-energy-assessments) in 6 categories: air leaks/envelope, insulation, heating & cooling, lighting, appliances/electronics, and water heating. Commercial mode adapts the same 6 categories for non-residential building types.

What assessors learn & demonstrate

  • Reading a building envelope (heat loss, thermal bridging, air leakage)
  • Identifying HVAC efficiency opportunities (setbacks, filter maintenance, controls)
  • Lighting load assessment (incandescent/CFL/LED, occupancy controls, daylighting)
  • Standby/phantom load identification (plug loads, vampire devices)
  • Documenting findings in field conditions and producing professional reports
  • Connecting technical findings to environmental justice context
  • Connecting clients to available financial assistance and weatherization programs

Methodology notes

  • The draft-detection method used is the DOE-approved hand-feel technique (a simpler alternative to the formal pressurization/blower-door test reserved for credentialed auditors).
  • The Heat Vision tool is a software-only false-color simulation, not a calibrated IR camera. It teaches the principle and helps locate temperature differences but should be paired with manual confirmation.
  • Savings estimates use national averages (residential: ~16.7¢/kWh electric, mid-range natural-gas heating; commercial: per-square-foot percentage models). Actual savings vary significantly by local utility rates, climate zone, site age, occupancy, and existing efficiency.

Grade-level expectations

  • Grades 6–8: Pair with cohort lead; focus on observation, documentation, and resident/owner interview.
  • Grades 9–10: Independent walk-through with cohort lead review of reports before delivery.
  • Grades 11–12: Lead assessor; capable of running the full walk-through, generating & delivering the report, and presenting findings to the client.

National resource connection list

  • DOE Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP) — Free weatherization for income-eligible households in every state. Find your state office: energy.gov/scep/wap
  • LIHEAP — Federal heating/cooling bill assistance, administered by states. National clearinghouse: liheapch.acf.hhs.gov
  • 211 — Dial 2-1-1 anywhere in the U.S. for connection to local utility assistance, weatherization, and emergency aid
  • ENERGY STAR — Federal rebates, tax credits, and product information: energystar.gov
  • DSIRE — Database of every state and local incentive for energy efficiency: dsireusa.org
  • Your local utility — Most U.S. utilities offer free or rebated weatherization, LED bulbs, smart thermostats, and audits for income-eligible customers. Look for "Energy Efficiency Programs" on the utility's website.

Privacy & data

  • Reports are generated locally on the assessor's device.
  • When an assessor sends a report by email, a copy is automatically CC'd to info@younggiftedgreen.org for YGG's program records. The "View / Print" option keeps everything offline.
  • If the resident or business opts in to follow-up, their info is shared only for program outreach (connecting them to their state's Weatherization Assistance Program, LIHEAP, local utility programs, etc.).
  • The Heat Vision tool processes the camera feed locally; no images are uploaded.

🔦 Light Leak Finder

At night, turn off interior lights. Press your phone (screen facing OUT) against the edge of a closed door, window, or attic hatch.

An observer on the other side watches for any light shining through cracks. Light leaks = air leaks.
